## Q1 — How do the 17 OKR groups relate to the 5 departments? *(the keystone — answer this first)*

The framework gives 5 departments. The OKR sheet has 17 groups under a company line. They don't line up 1:1, and the whole hierarchy depends on which is the "real" backbone.

**Ask it like this:**
- *Tee it up with the screen:* "When we show the company on one screen, what are the boxes under the company line — your 5 departments, or these 17 teams? And does every OKR hang off exactly one box?"
- *Give them a multiple choice (decide, don't philosophize):*
  - **A)** The 17 groups *are* the department level for OKRs (the 5 are just for tasks/NCRs).
  - **B)** The 5 departments are the parents; the 17 groups nest underneath them.
  - **C)** The 17 are just team/owner labels; every OKR still attaches to one of the 5 departments.

**You've got a real answer when:** they pick A, B, or C **and** every one of the 17 groups has a named home — no "we'll figure that out later." (e.g. "B — Flowback Repair and Frac Repair sit under Flowback; Inside Sales and Marketing under Business Team…" for all 17.)

**Capture it here:**
- Structure chosen (A/B/C): ______
- All 17 groups placed? Y/N. If B/C, the mapping: __________________________
