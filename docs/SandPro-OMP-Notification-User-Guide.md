# SandPro OMP Notification User Guide

## The Simple Version

SandPro OMP uses three kinds of notifications:

1. **Daily email:** One morning summary email per person.
2. **Phone or computer push:** A timely alert when something needs attention.
3. **App bell:** A saved list of notifications inside SandPro OMP.

During the pilot, daily email is sent only to Andrew, Jake, Merci, and Tim. Push notifications do not use the Resend email quota.

## What Sends a Push Notification

You may receive a push notification when:

- Someone assigns a task or objective to you.
- Someone tags you or adds you to an objective.
- Someone mentions you in a message.
- Someone comments on work you own or follow.
- Work assigned to you is due soon.
- Work assigned to you becomes overdue.
- Work you own or follow is marked blocked or at risk.
- Someone acknowledges work you delegated.
- A Fix-It item that matters to you is created or updated.

Tapping a push notification opens the related objective, message, or Fix-It item in SandPro OMP.

## Daily Email Rule

- The daily brief is the only normal email from SandPro OMP.
- Each pilot user can receive no more than one daily brief email per day.
- Assignments, comments, mentions, reminders, and risk alerts do not send email.
- Those work alerts use push notifications and the app bell.
- If the daily email cannot be sent, SandPro OMP may use push as a fallback.

## Turn On Push on iPhone

1. Open `objectivetracker.net` in Safari.
2. Tap the Share button.
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**.
4. Open SandPro OMP from the new Home Screen icon.
5. Open **Settings** in SandPro OMP.
6. Find **Push Notification Setup**.
7. Tap **Enable push**.
8. Tap **Allow** when the iPhone asks for permission.

Push notifications will not work on iPhone unless SandPro OMP is opened from the Home Screen app.

## Turn On Push on Android

1. Open `objectivetracker.net` in Chrome.
2. Use Chrome's menu to install the app or add it to the Home Screen.
3. Open the installed SandPro OMP app.
4. Open **Settings** in SandPro OMP.
5. Find **Push Notification Setup**.
6. Tap **Enable push**.
7. Tap **Allow** when Android asks for permission.

## Turn On Push on a Computer

1. Open `objectivetracker.net` in a supported browser.
2. Open **Settings** in SandPro OMP.
3. Find **Push Notification Setup**.
4. Tap **Enable push**.
5. Allow notifications when the browser asks.

Push is enabled separately on each phone, tablet, or computer. Turning it on for one device does not automatically turn it on for another.

## Change Notification Preferences

Open **Settings**, then **Notification Preferences**.

- **Daily Brief Email:** Turns the one morning email on or off.
- **Due Reminders:** Controls due-soon push alerts.
- **Overdue Alerts:** Controls overdue push alerts.
- **Blocker Notifications:** Controls blocked and at-risk push alerts.
- **Comment Notifications:** Controls comment and mention push alerts.
- **Delegation Alerts:** Controls assignment and delegation push alerts.
- **Daily Brief Cadence:** Sets the morning email to daily, weekly, or off.

The app bell remains the saved record of notifications even when a phone alert is missed. Fix-It updates also remain visible in the Fix-It Feed.

## If Push Is Not Working

Check these items in order:

1. Open SandPro OMP on the device that should receive the alert.
2. Go to **Settings**, then **Push Notification Setup**.
3. Confirm it says **Enabled on this device**.
4. Check the phone or browser settings and confirm SandPro OMP notifications are allowed.
5. Check Focus, Do Not Disturb, or battery-saving settings.
6. If the subscription expired, tap **Disable**, then **Enable push** again.
7. On iPhone, confirm you are using the Home Screen app and not a normal Safari tab.

If the app bell shows the notification but the phone does not, the work notification was created correctly and the device permission or push subscription needs attention.

## Privacy Note

Push alerts may show an objective title or short work message on the lock screen. Each user can control lock-screen previews in the phone's notification settings.
